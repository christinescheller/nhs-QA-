Northshore High School

What is PBIS?

At Northshore, PBIS is a school-wide intervention program that has two main focuses: 1) reward students for appropriate behavior, and 2) bring attention to counter-productive behavior in an effort to extinguish the behavior.

What is appropriate behavior

Appropriate behavior can be as simple as being dress code compliant, having homework completed, or holding a door for someone. It can also be the demonstration of extra academic effort such as leading a class discussion, tutoring another student, or making marked improvement in a subject.

What are the rewards?

Students who receive Panther Bucks from faculty members for appropriate behavior can cash them in every Friday at the Panther Pantry located in the main courtyard. The pantry is stocked with goodies that kids love . . . candy, chips, breakfast bars, and baked goods. Students who redeem their Panther Bucks are also entered into a drawing for a “Front of the Line” pass that allows students to cut the lunch line. Two passes are drawn monthly. Donut Days are another way we reinforce good behavior. Several times a month we pop into a class unannounced. If everyone in class is dress code compliant then the class gets to enjoy hot glazed donuts courtesy of PBIS. The Purr-fect Panther program rewards students for coming to school and being on time. Students who have perfect attendance, no tardies, and no discipline are invited to a pizza party at the end of each semester.

How do we determine negative behaviors that need attention?

We have a PBIS committee that meets once a month to review discipline data. We also complete a comprehensive attendance and discipline data analysis at the end of each year. The committee consists of teachers, paraprofessionals, counselors, and administration.

What negative behaviors are we focused on this year?

This year we’re focusing on attendance and bullying.

Every Day Counts!

Research is clear: students are more likely to succeed academically when they attend school consistently. It is difficult for the teacher and the class to build their skills and progress if a large number of students are frequently absent or come late to class. Coming to school every day and on time teaches students life skills that carry over to their personal and work life. Over the past several years, school attendance has declined at Northshore. While missing a few days here and there might not seem like a big deal, the cumulative effect has a negative impact:

School Year Total Absences Average Per Student Instructional Hours Missed
2016-17 14,836 9.3 66
2015-16 14,389 8.9 62

This year we are focusing on improving student attendance and reducing tardies. Beyond improved academic achievement, we have designed the following incentives to motivate students to come to school:

Every 9 Weeks

By Grade:
  • Class with lowest percentage of tardies
  • Class with lowest percentage of absences

For Individuals
  • No tardies – 1 homework pass
  • No absences – 2 homework passes
  • No tardies or absences – 2 homework passes, 1 free dress Friday
  • No tardies, absences, or discipline – 2 homework passes, 1 free dress Friday, 1 event pass

Semester:

For Individuals
  • No tardies – 1 homework pass
  • No absences – 2 free dress Fridays
  • No tardies or absences – 2 homework passes, 2 free dress Fridays, 2 event passes
  • No tardies, absences, or discipline – 2 homework passes, 2 free dress Fridays, 2 event passes, and APP pizza party

Please check back monthly to see where your child’s class stands in the rankings! Working together, we can improve school attendance and our students’ chances for success.
