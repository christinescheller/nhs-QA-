History

Northshore High School in Slidell, Louisiana, serves a population of approximately 14,000 in their district. Currently, there are 1,632 students enrolled, which is about a 10 percent increase over the last four years. The students of Northshore High are residents of a suburban community just east and across the lake from the city of New Orleans. Slidell’s population is just over 29,000.

Northshore’s district encompasses some Slidell residents, as well as a majority of residents residing outside the city limits of Slidell proper. The city has shown major growth in the last few years. This growth has been about 500 new residents per year, with an average of three members per household. Projections indicate that the growth for the area should remain constant. Northshore High School is a public high school is a public high school serving the eastern end of St. Tammany Parish and the city of Slidell, Louisiana.

The school was incorporated in 1982 and was the fourth high school to serve the clientele in the Slidell area. It began as a school for approximately 800 students and has increased in population to its present level. The school year follows the St. Tammy Parish School Board calendar and provides 178 instructional days for ninth through twelfth grades. Uniforms were adopted for Northshore High School students beginning with the 1999-2000 school year. A majority vote by all parents was required in order to establish a uniform policy. A team composed of parents, teachers, and students chose the style and colors of the uniform clothes. Facilities have changed over the 33 years since the initial construction of Northshore High. As population increased, additional wings have been added to the main building.

Northshore offers an extensive selection of courses. There is a wide range of choices for electives. Most students are encouraged to complete a college line of studies. The major defining features and characteristics of Northshore High are academic excellence, high scoring testing student body, the variety and award winning excellence of the fine arts program, and improvement to campus facilities.

Northshore High School Mission Statement

A challenging and caring community encouraging lifelong learning for all students.

Vision Statement

The Northshore High School administration, faculty, and staff, including students, parents and community stakeholders will work together as a learning community engaged in a continuous process of strategic planning, shared decision-making, implemention of research based learning initiatives, and assessment of student performance to promote high student academic achievement and lifelong learning for all students.

Statement of Beliefs

  • Student learning is the chief priority for the school
  • A safe and physically comfortable environment promotes student learning.
  • Teachers, administrators, parents, and the community share the responsibility for advancing the school's mission.
  • Each student is a valued individual with unique physical, social, emotional, and intellectual needs.
  • Student involvement in the arts and respect for cultural diversity enhances academic performance in all of the discipline.
  • A student's self-esteem is enhanced by positive relationships and mutual respect among and between students and staff.
  • Students need to demonstrate their understanding of essential knowledge and skills, and need to be actively engaged in solving problems and producing quality work.
  • Students should be provided with a variety of instructional approaches that demonstrate our commitment to continuous improvement and that insure they become confident, self-directed, lifelong learners.
